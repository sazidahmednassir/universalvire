What is group psychotherapy?
Group psychotherapy is a type of psychological therapy where a group of individuals facing similar psychological challenges work together under the supervision of specialists in a safe therapeutic environment. It provides participants with the opportunity to connect with others experiencing similar issues in a secure setting, fostering a sense of safety, mutual support, and understanding.
How many people are in each group therapy session?
The maximum number of participants is typically 12. This number allows sufficient time for each individual to interact and participate.
Is group psychotherapy suitable for me?
If you are experiencing disorders such as anxiety or depression or struggling to cope with daily life pressures and relationships, group psychotherapy may be an excellent option for you.
Is group psychotherapy as effective as individual psychotherapy?
Yes, group psychotherapy is highly effective for addressing many psychological issues, such as anxiety, loss, depression, and adjustment disorders. Unlike individual therapy, it relies on group interaction and participation, fostering social support that enhances the therapys effectiveness.
